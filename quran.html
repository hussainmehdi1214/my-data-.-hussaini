Qur'an ulqareem

The Qur'an is the holy book of Islam. It is believed by Muslims to be the literal word of God (Allah), revealed to the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) over a period of 23 years.

Key Facts about the Qur'an:

  1. Language: Originally revealed in classical Arabic.
  2. Chapters & Verses: It has 114 chapters, called Surahs, and over 6,000 verses, called Ayahs.
  3. Revealed by: The angel Jibreel (Gabriel) to Prophet Muhammad (PBUH).
  4. Message: It contains guidance for life, including:
    • Belief in one God
    • Worship and prayer
    • Moral conduct
    • Laws for society
    • Stories of past prophets
    • The Day of Judgment

Qur’an and Science:

The Qur’an contains verses that reflect on natural phenomena, encouraging humans to think and reflect. Many scientists have found scientific facts in the Qur’an that were discovered centuries later.

Respect for the Qur'an:

  • Muslims must perform ablution (wudu) before touching or reciting it.
  • It is kept in a clean and high place
  • It is never to be disrespected or thrown.
